mansaf

is a traditional Arab dish made of lamb cooked in a sauce of fermented dried yogurt and served with rice or bulgur.

ingredients

  • rice
  • lamb meat
  • jameed
  • almonds

how to make it

  1. In a large pot over medium high heat, add one kilo, or two pounds of chopped leg of lamb into boiling water.
  2. Add enough water to cover the lamb, once boiling reduce the heat, and cook slowly until the lamb is cooked.
  3. Make rice, and reconstitute the jameed (dry yoghurt) by breaking it into pieces and soaking it in water.
  4. Using a food processor, process the jameed and water to make a thick paste.
  5. Drain the meat using a sieve and reserve the brothy liquid the meat was cooked in.
  6. Mix the jameed paste with the broth and stir until you have a creamy white sauce
